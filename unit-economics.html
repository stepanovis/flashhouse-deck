What is a hypothesis — and how it gets measured

  • Cost to serve — token usage per agent, measured directly in the 3 design-partner pilots (first 60 days).
  • Churn — weekly active usage as the leading indicator; month-2/3 cohort retention as proof.
  • CAC $800 — planning number for the salaried-bizdev motion; validated with the first hire. Founder-led sales today are near-zero cash CAC.
  • Price $300 — accepted by 3 design partners (discounted pilots); list-price validation on the next 10 non-partner sales.

What is already known

  • 3 design partners committed to pilot and pay — willingness to pay exists.
  • The engine is live — Keepstar runs in production (2–4s responses, data never passes through the LLM).
  • The lift is real — the same automation playbook at Homeapp took agent conversion 0.2 → 1.2 deals/mo (6×).
  • Cost structure is controllable — start on aggregators (zero capex), own inference at scale → margin grows.

The operating rhythm

Weekly: each active bet ships 1–3 micro-experiments → resolved bets flip to confirmed (1) or killed (0) → the forecast tightens toward reality. After ~2 months we replace the 20% assumption with our measured hit rate — the forecast becomes self-correcting.
